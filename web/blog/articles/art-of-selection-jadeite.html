Reading the stone before the mark

A gemstone seal begins long before a character is drawn. The artisan first studies density, translucency, internal fracture lines, and the way color moves through the material. Jadeite rewards patience: the most compelling pieces carry depth without becoming opaque, and they hold a clean edge when the blade meets the surface.

Why density matters

The stone must be firm enough to keep fine strokes sharp, yet balanced enough to absorb the rhythm of hand carving. A seal that looks beautiful but crumbles at the corners cannot become a daily instrument. Selection is therefore both aesthetic and practical.

For STONE SIGNATURE, the right material is one that can carry a name with quiet permanence. Color, weight, and grain are chosen to support the final impression, not to compete with it.
