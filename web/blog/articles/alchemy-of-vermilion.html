The color of confirmation

Vermilion has long carried the visual weight of authority. On paper, its red is immediate and unmistakable, separating a sealed decision from ordinary writing. The impression is both color and declaration.

Pigment, oil, and patience

Traditional seal paste is not only pigment. It depends on mineral color, binding oil, and fibers that help the paste hold its body. The result must cling to fine carved lines without flooding them, then transfer cleanly to paper.

A finished seal reaches its full expression only when stone, carving, paste, and paper work together. Vermilion is the final material that lets the carved name appear with force.
